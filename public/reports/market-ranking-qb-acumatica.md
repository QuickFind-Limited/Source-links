## Executive Summary

The QB → Acumatica corridor is **vendor-dominated** to a degree not seen in other corridors. Acumatica.com itself appears in ~48 of 55 queries (87%), with industry-specific landing pages for Distribution, Construction, Manufacturing, and MEP contractors. This is a **100% channel model** — all implementations go through certified VARs (Value Added Resellers), creating a fragmented partner ecosystem of 20+ active firms competing for migration work.

**Key Differentiator**: Acumatica's **unlimited user licensing** (consumption-based pricing by resource usage, not per-seat) is the single most repeated selling point across all content. This fundamentally changes the TCO conversation vs. NetSuite, D365, and Sage Intacct.

**Market Opportunity**: Despite Acumatica's strong vendor content, there is **zero independent comparison content** and **no AI-specific migration content**. The partner ecosystem produces mostly thin, derivative blog posts. Source can differentiate by producing the deep, data-driven content that doesn't exist yet.

## Pricing Intelligence

### Acumatica Licensing Model
- **Consumption-based pricing** — Pay by resource usage (transactions, storage, compute), NOT per user
- **Unlimited users** included in all plans — the #1 differentiator vs. NetSuite/D365/Intacct
- **Three deployment options**: SaaS (Acumatica-hosted), Private Cloud Subscription, Perpetual License
- **Perpetual license**: One-time purchase + annual maintenance (15-25% of license cost)
- **SaaS subscription**: Annual fee based on usage tier

### Pricing Ranges (from SERP data)
| Component | Range | Notes |
|-----------|-------|-------|
| Essentials Edition (annual) | ~$7,000/yr | Entry-level, small business |

| Implementation services | $25,000-$150,000 | Varies dramatically by scope |

## Competitive Landscape: QB → Acumatica vs. Other Corridors

### Unique Characteristics
1. **100% Channel Model**: Unlike NetSuite (mix of direct + partner) or Sage Intacct (strong direct sales), ALL Acumatica implementations go through certified VARs. This creates:
   - More partner-generated content (but mostly thin/derivative)
   - No single dominant implementation partner
   - Price variability across partners for similar scope
   
2. **Unlimited User Pricing**: Fundamentally different from NetSuite ($99-199/user/mo) and D365 ($70-100/user/mo). Changes the TCO math entirely for companies with 20+ users.

3. **Industry-Specific Editions**: Acumatica has dedicated editions (General Business, Distribution, Manufacturing, Construction, Retail-Commerce) with separate migration paths. Other ERPs handle verticals through modules.

4. **Vendor Content Dominance**: Acumatica.com produces more targeted migration content than any other vendor in our corridors. They have dedicated QB migration pages per industry vertical, comparison charts, downloadable guides, and webinar series.

5. **ERP Rescue Niche**: This is the only corridor with dedicated "failed implementation rescue" firms (Variux, KALM Consulting). Suggests implementation failure rate is notable enough to create a market.

### Cross-Corridor Overlap
- **Acumatica vs NetSuite vs Intacct**: Heavy comparison content. All three compete for the "outgrown QB" segment.
- **Partners overlap**: NexTec, SWK Technologies, and a few others also appear in QB→NetSuite and QB→Intacct corridors.
