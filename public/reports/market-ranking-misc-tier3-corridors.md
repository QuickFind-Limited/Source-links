## Executive Summary

These three corridors represent the "long tail" of ERP migration — smaller addressable markets but with unique characteristics that make them strategically interesting for Source:

1. **Dynamics SL → D365 BC**: ~13,500 captive users facing EOL (extended support ends July 2028, subscriptions end April 2031). Project accounting-focused. Acumatica is aggressively targeting this corridor with dedicated migration tooling — more so than Microsoft's own D365 BC path.

2. **MYOB → NetSuite**: AU/NZ-specific corridor. MYOB serves 1.2M+ active businesses across ANZ. MYOB Acumatica (rebranded from MYOB Advanced) is the local competitor to NetSuite. MYOB Exo is legacy/on-premise approaching EOL. Geo-specific opportunity for Source's ANZ expansion.

3. **FreshBooks → NetSuite**: Micro-to-mid transition corridor. FreshBooks has ~3,500 enterprise users but serves millions of freelancers/small businesses. The "outgrown FreshBooks" signal is strong — firms hit client limits (5-50 per plan), user limits (1 per plan), and lack of inventory/multi-entity. Migration cost is $50K-150K first year. Very thin competitive content.

**Combined Opportunity Score: 7.5/10** — Low volume individually but collectively represent underserved niches where Source can dominate with minimal competition.
