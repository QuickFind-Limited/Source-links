## Executive Summary

The Sage 200/300 → NetSuite corridor is a **distinctly UK/EU-centric market** with unique dynamics compared to US-focused corridors. Key findings:

- **Sage 200 Manufacturing EOL is the biggest forced migration catalyst** — module removed in 2025 R2, support ended Dec 31, 2025, creating urgent migration demand
- **NetSuite aggressively targets Sage users** with its "Sage Switch Program" (50% off first year, free data migration up to £5,000) and dedicated UK landing page claiming 500+ companies switched
- **The market is dominated by UK-based boutique consultancies** rather than large SIs — Eventura, NoBlue, Cogent2, Threadgold Consulting, TSG
- **Data migration specialists** (SwitchMyBooks, Cloud-Accounting.io) are a unique player type in this corridor
- **Sage 200 caps at 50 users** — this hard limit creates a natural migration trigger for growing businesses
- **Migration timelines are 9-14 months** realistically (vs. vendor claims of "weeks"), with 30% budget contingency recommended
- **No competitor offers AI-assisted or fixed-price migration** — Source's positioning gap is wide open

**Opportunity Score: 8.0/10** — Strong opportunity driven by forced manufacturing EOL migration, 50-user cap ceiling, and fragmented consultant landscape with no dominant player.

### Five Unique Characteristics

1. **Sage competes with itself** — Sage actively promotes Intacct as the upgrade path from Sage 200, while NetSuite targets the same users. This creates a three-way battle: Sage 200 retention vs. Sage Intacct upgrade vs. NetSuite switch.

2. **Manufacturing EOL creates urgency** — Unlike other corridors where migration is optional, Sage 200 Manufacturing users are forced to act by Dec 31, 2025 deadline.

3. **Data migration specialists dominate** — SwitchMyBooks and Cloud-Accounting.io (with dedicated Sage 200 AND Sage 300 migration pages) are a player type unique to this corridor.

4. **UK compliance is a key factor** — VAT, Making Tax Digital, HMRC reporting, iXBRL filing — these UK-specific requirements mean migration partners need UK expertise.

5. **Sage 300 is a separate sub-market** — Sage 300 (formerly Accpac) has a different geographic base (Canada, international), different technical architecture, and different migration tooling (Sage 300 Data Migration Utility).
