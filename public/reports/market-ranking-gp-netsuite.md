## Executive Summary

The GP→NetSuite corridor is a **high-opportunity, actively contested market** driven by Microsoft's announcement of Dynamics GP end-of-life (mainstream support ending 2028, security patches ending April 2031). An estimated 40,000+ organizations still run Dynamics GP, creating a massive forced-migration wave. Unlike the GP→D365 BC corridor (where Microsoft pushes its own successor), the GP→NetSuite corridor represents **cross-platform defection** — GP users choosing Oracle's cloud ERP over staying in the Microsoft ecosystem.

**Key finding:** This corridor is dominated by a unique player — **eOne Solutions** — which operates not as a traditional consultancy but as a **migration tooling vendor** (SmartConnect, Popdock). eOne owns the "how to migrate data" conversation more completely than any single firm in any other corridor we've researched. Meanwhile, the consultancy landscape is fragmented across 15+ firms, with no single implementation partner achieving dominant SERP authority.

**Source opportunity score: 8.5/10** — The corridor has strong search volume, fragmented consultancy competition, excellent content gaps (especially around AI migration, cost calculators, and GP→NS vs GP→D365 BC comparison content), and a time-pressured audience with a 2028-2029 deadline.

## Pricing Intelligence

### Dynamics GP Pricing (Legacy)
- **Perpetual License:** $5,000–$10,000 per user (one-time, historical)
- **Annual Enhancement Plan:** ~16% of license cost
- **On-premise infrastructure:** $5,000–$25,000/year for servers, SQL licensing, IT maintenance
- **GP ISV add-ons:** $2,000–$15,000 each (many have no NetSuite equivalent)
- **Total 5-year TCO (GP on-prem, 10 users):** $100,000–$175,000

### Oracle NetSuite Pricing
- **Platform fee:** $999/month base
- **Per-user fee:** $99/month per named user
- **SuiteSuccess editions:** Premium pricing for industry-specific bundles
- **Implementation:** $25,000–$150,000+ depending on complexity
- **Total 5-year TCO (NetSuite, 10 users):** $150,000–$250,000+ (per stockton10.com analysis)

### D365 Business Central Pricing (Competitor Corridor)
- **Per-user:** $70/user/month (Essentials) or $100/user/month (Premium)
- **Microsoft offers 40% discount for GP users** migrating to D365 BC
- **Total 5-year TCO (BC, 10 users):** ~$128,000 (per stockton10.com)
- **Key insight:** BC costs roughly half of NetSuite over 5 years, even with higher implementation cost

### GP → NetSuite Migration Costs
- **Simple migration (< 10 users, basic financials):** $50,000–$100,000
- **Mid-market (10-50 users, manufacturing/distribution):** $100,000–$250,000
- **Enterprise (50+ users, multi-subsidiary, complex):** $250,000–$500,000+
- **eOne migration tools licensing:** Additional $5,000–$15,000 for SmartConnect + Popdock
- **Timeline:** 4–12 months typical (lower complexity ~6 months per i-techsupport.com)

### Source Opportunity
- **Source target price:** $5,000–$15,000 (AI-assisted, fixed-price)
- **Market disruption potential:** 90–97% cheaper than traditional migration
- **Key selling point:** GP users facing $100K+ migration costs with 2028 deadline = price-sensitive, time-pressured audience

## Key Dynamics of This Corridor

### 1. GP End-of-Life Creates Massive Urgency
- Microsoft announced no new enhancements, regulatory updates, or technical support after Dec 31, 2029
- Security patches end April 30, 2031
- **~40,000 organizations** still on GP must migrate somewhere
- Microsoft pushes D365 Business Central with 40% discount — NetSuite must fight for share

### 2. The "GP→NS vs GP→D365 BC" Decision Point
- This is THE critical content gap. Every GP user must decide: stay Microsoft or go Oracle?
- Current SERP results are fragmented — no single authoritative comparison
- stockton10.com and erpsoftwareblog.com have partial TCO comparisons
- **Source opportunity:** Create the definitive "GP→NS vs GP→D365 BC" comparison guide

### 3. eOne Solutions is a Unique Market Dynamic
- Not a consultancy — a migration tool vendor used BY consultancies
- Their no-code toolkit (SmartConnect + Popdock) is the de facto standard for GP→NS data migration
- Historical data archival is a solved problem if you use eOne tools
- **Implication for Source:** Must position around eOne tools or compete with them. Partnership potential.

### 4. Dual-Platform Partners Have Credibility Advantage
- Sikich and Rand Group both hold Microsoft AND Oracle/NetSuite partnerships
- They can advise on the GP→D365 vs GP→NS decision credibly
- **Source angle:** Position as system-agnostic advisor with AI-powered cost analysis across both paths

### 5. Content is Migration-Rich, Unlike SAP B1→NS
- Unlike SAP B1→NS (which was "comparison-saturated, migration-starved"), GP→NS has **dedicated migration content** from 10+ firms
- The gap is in **differentiated content** — AI migration, cost calculators, industry-specific guides, video content
