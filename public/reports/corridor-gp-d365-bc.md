# Competitive Intelligence Report: Dynamics GP → D365 Business Central Migration Corridor

**Date:** 2026-03-16
**Methodology:** 55 SERP queries (Firecrawl), 15 competitor page scrapes, 5 autonomous research agents
**Corridor Status:** CRITICAL — Microsoft ending GP support Dec 31, 2029; captive audience of ~40,000 GP customers

---

## 1. Executive Summary

The Dynamics GP to Business Central migration corridor is one of the most concentrated ERP migration opportunities in the market. Microsoft's September 2024 announcement of GP end-of-life (mainstream support ending December 31, 2029, security patches ending April 30, 2031) has created a massive captive audience of forced migrations. The competitive landscape is dominated by a handful of well-positioned Microsoft partners who have built dedicated migration practices, proprietary tooling, and aggressive content strategies.

**Key findings:**
- **40,000+ GP customers** must migrate before 2029, creating a multi-billion dollar opportunity
- **Rand Group** dominates commercial/transactional SERP with published pricing and a diagnostic-first sales approach
- **RSM** is the largest player by revenue (top 3 BC reseller worldwide, 1,300+ Microsoft professionals)
- **Simplanova** owns the fixed-price calculator niche with 600+ completed projects
- **eOne Solutions** dominates the migration tooling space with SmartConnect/Popdock
- **Microsoft's own tools** (Cloud Migration Setup, Bridge to the Cloud 3) are significant factors
- **Pricing ranges from $9,999 (Brightpoint) to $125K+ (RSM)** with most mid-market projects in the $50K-$150K range
- **Content gap opportunity:** Very few competitors address AI/Copilot integration, post-migration optimization, or industry-specific migration playbooks

## 7. Microsoft Migration Tooling & Incentives

### Official Microsoft Tools

#### 1. Cloud Migration Setup (GP to BC)
- Built into Business Central
- Supports GP 2015 and later
- Migrates: Chart of accounts, customers, vendors, items, open transactions, historical snapshots
- **Limitations:** No custom table support, no ISV add-on migration, requires Azure Data Factory self-hosted runtime, limited error handling
- **Best for:** Simple, standard GP environments

#### 2. Configuration Packages
- Manual data mapping and import
- More flexible than Cloud Migration Setup
- Supports custom fields and some customizations
- **Limitations:** Difficult to customize, limited error handling, no file/attachment migration
- **Best for:** Semi-complex migrations with partner expertise

#### 3. Data Migration Wizard
- Basic GP data import
- Only works with newer GP versions
- Requires extensive manual cleanup
- **Limitations:** Leaves out critical historical data, minimal automation

### Microsoft Incentive Programs

#### Bridge to the Cloud 3 (BTC3)
- **Active:** January 2026 - December 2027
- **Discount:** 30% on BC licensing with 3-year commitment
- **Target:** Dynamics GP, NAV, SL, AX customers
- **Previous versions:** BTC1 (60%), BTC2 (40%), BTC3 (30%)

## 10. Forum & Community Sentiment

### Reddit / Community.Dynamics.com Themes
Based on SERP captures of discussion threads:

1. **Migration anxiety is high** — GP users worried about losing customizations, learning curve, data integrity
2. **"BC is not GP in the cloud"** — Repeated warning in community discussions; users frustrated by partners who oversimplify
3. **Historical data is the #1 concern** — Users want full transaction history accessible in BC, not archived separately
4. **ISV replacement headaches** — Many GP add-ons have no BC equivalent; users struggling with gaps
5. **Licensing cost shock** — Moving from perpetual GP licenses to monthly BC subscriptions creates sticker shock
6. **Timeline frustration** — Users expect weeks, reality is months to years for complex environments
7. **BTC3 awareness growing** — More discussions about how to leverage the 30% discount
8. **Alternative consideration** — Some GP users evaluating Sage Intacct, NetSuite, or Acumatica instead of BC
