# SAGE → NETSUITE: MARKET INTELLIGENCE REPORT

**Date**: 2026-03-16
**Corridor**: Sage 50/100 → Oracle NetSuite
**Methodology**: 55 SERP queries (550 results) across 4 intent clusters, 15 competitor page scrapes, 5 autonomous research agents
**Source**: Firecrawl SERP + Scrape + Agent Pipeline

---

## 1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

The Sage-to-NetSuite migration corridor is a **high-value, mid-competition market** dominated by a mix of Oracle's own properties, boutique ERP consultancies, and data conversion specialists. Unlike the QuickBooks→D365 corridor (saturated by Microsoft content) or GP→D365 (dominated by legacy partner networks), this corridor has **significant content gaps and fragmented authority**, creating clear opportunities for a well-positioned entrant.

**Key Findings**:
- **No single dominant authority** — the top domain (oracle.com/netsuite.com) captures only ~18% of SERP real estate across all intents
- **Boutique consultancies dominate commercial/transactional SERPs** — firms like Jade Global, BrokenRubik, and cloud-accounting.io outperform larger brands
- **Pricing content is extremely scarce** — only 2-3 pages provide specific Sage→NetSuite migration cost data
- **UK market is underserved** — only netsuite.co.uk and nolanbusinesssolutions.co.uk serve this geography
- **Reddit/forum content ranks highly** for informational queries, indicating Google values authentic user experiences
- **Data conversion specialists** (MMC Convert, SwitchMyBooks, Clone Partner) own a niche but lack authority content
- **"Sage upgrade path" queries** reveal users considering Sage Intacct as alternative to NetSuite — dual positioning opportunity

## 7. SAGE UPGRADE PATH ANALYSIS

### The Three Paths for Sage 100 Users

```
                    ┌─────────────────┐
                    │   Sage 100      │
                    │   (On-Premise)  │
                    └───────┬─────────┘
                            │
              ┌─────────────┼─────────────┐
              ▼             ▼             ▼
    ┌─────────────┐ ┌─────────────┐ ┌─────────────┐
    │ Path 1:     │ │ Path 2:     │ │ Path 3:     │
    │ Sage Intacct│ │ NetSuite    │ │ Other ERP   │
    │ (Sage Cloud)│ │ (Oracle)    │ │ (D365, etc) │
    └─────────────┘ └─────────────┘ └─────────────┘
```

**Path 1: Sage Intacct** (Sage's own cloud offering)
- Easiest data migration (same vendor ecosystem)
- Lower initial cost ($425/mo base)
- Best for: accounting-centric orgs, <$50M revenue
- Limitation: weaker on manufacturing, inventory, ecommerce
- Sage is aggressively marketing this path

**Path 2: NetSuite** (Most common migration destination)
- Most comprehensive platform (ERP + CRM + ecommerce)
- Higher cost but broader functionality
- Best for: growth companies, multi-entity, multi-currency, manufacturing
- SuiteSuccess reduces implementation risk
- Dominant in SERP results for "Sage migration" queries

**Path 3: Other ERPs** (D365 Business Central, Acumatica, etc.)
- D365 BC for Microsoft-centric organizations
- Acumatica gaining share in manufacturing
- Minimal SERP presence for Sage-specific migration content
- **Gap**: No authoritative "Sage 100 to D365" content (separate corridor)

### Sage End-of-Life Signals
- Sage has not formally announced Sage 100 end of life
- However, investment in Sage Intacct (cloud) signals long-term direction
- On-premise Sage 100 updates becoming less frequent
- Partner ecosystem gradually shifting to Intacct
- **SERP signal**: Growing query volume for "Sage 100 end of life" and "Sage 100 upgrade options" — users are anticipating the transition
